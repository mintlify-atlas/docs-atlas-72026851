---
title: "Sharing your collections and media entries with others"
description: "Generate shareable links for collections and individual entries, protect them with a password or email invite list, and control who can view your content."
---

Movie Circle lets you share both entire collections and individual media entries with anyone — or with a specific set of people. Every shareable item gets a unique public URL under the `/share/` route. You control access through three modes: fully public (anyone with the link), password-protected, or invitation-only (restricted to specific email addresses). You can change or revoke these settings at any time from the sharing settings dialog.

## Sharing a collection

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the collection">
    Go to `/collections/[id]` for the collection you want to share.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open sharing settings">
    Click the **Share** button in the collection header. The sharing settings dialog opens.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Copy the share link">
    The dialog shows the share link in a read-only field. Click the **Copy** icon to copy it to your clipboard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set visibility">
    Choose **Public** (anyone with the link can view) or **Private** (restricted access). Public is selected by default.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save Settings** to apply your changes. The link is now active.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The share link for a collection follows this pattern:

```
https://your-domain.com/share/collection/[collection-id]
```

## Sharing an individual media entry

You can also share a single entry — one movie or TV show from a collection — as a standalone page.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the collection containing the entry">
    Navigate to `/collections/[id]`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the entry's share settings">
    Hover over the entry card and click the **Share** icon, or open the entry's edit dialog and click **Share** from there.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Copy the link and configure access">
    The sharing dialog works the same way as for collections. Copy the link and choose public, password, or invitation-only access.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Shared item links follow this pattern:

```
https://your-domain.com/share/item/[entry-id]
```

A shared item page displays the movie or TV show's TMDB information (title, poster, overview, genres, release date) together with the owner's personal rating and notes.

## Access control modes

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Public" icon="globe">
    Anyone who has the link can view the shared content without signing in. No password or account is required.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Password-protected" icon="lock">
    Visitors must enter the correct password before they can view the content. The password is set by you in the sharing settings dialog.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Invitation only" icon="mail">
    Access is restricted to a list of email addresses you specify. Visitors must be signed in to Movie Circle with a matching email address.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Private (no link active)" icon="eye-off">
    If you have not enabled sharing, the collection or entry is not accessible to anyone except you.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Password protection

When you select **Private → Password Only**, you set a password in the sharing settings dialog. Anyone who visits the share link — whether or not they have a Movie Circle account — sees a password prompt and must enter the correct password to proceed.

<Warning>
There is currently no way to recover a forgotten share password from the recipient's side. If you lose the password, you can overwrite it by opening the sharing settings dialog and setting a new one.
</Warning>

## Invitation-only access

When you select **Private → Invitation Only**, you add one or more email addresses to the invited list. Recipients must:

1. Have a Movie Circle account registered with that exact email address.
2. Be signed in when they visit the share link.

If a signed-in user's email is not on the invited list, they see an **Access Denied** message. If they are not signed in at all, they are prompted to sign in first.

<Note>
The collection or item owner always has access to their own shared content regardless of the invited email list.
</Note>

## How recipients access shared content

When a recipient opens a share link, Movie Circle checks access in the following order:

1. **No restrictions set** → content is shown immediately.
2. **Invitation-only** → the user must sign in; their email is checked against the invited list.
3. **Password-protected** → the user enters the password; a successful match grants access for the current browser session.

Shared collections at `/share/collection/[id]` display the owner's name, the collection name and description, and all media entries with their titles, posters, ratings, and notes. Each entry in a shared collection links to a media detail page at `/share/collection/[id]/media/[type]/[tmdbId]`, so recipients can explore individual titles in context.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I use both a password and an email invite list at the same time?">
    No. The sharing settings dialog treats password protection and invitation-only as mutually exclusive options. Choose one access method per item.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Does sharing a collection make it editable for recipients?">
    No. Shared links are always read-only. Recipients can view the collection and its entries but cannot add, edit, or remove anything.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I revoke a share link?">
    You can switch a collection or entry back to **Public** with no restrictions, or change the password or invited list at any time from the sharing settings dialog. Changing to a stricter access mode takes effect immediately for anyone who tries to load the link again.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do recipients need a Movie Circle account for public links?">
    No. Public share links work for anyone, including users who do not have a Movie Circle account. Only invitation-only links require recipients to sign in.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
